French Fries

Description:

Artisan fries are a type of french fry made with thicker cut potatoes, deep fried and tossed in herbs, spices, and other flavorings. They come in different shapes and sizes and can be served both hot or cold. Common spice combinations include garlic-Parmesan, rosemary-thyme, Cajun, and even sweet potato fries. Artisan fries have a more robust texture than traditional French fries, with a crunchy outside that gives way to a flaky, tender inside. They should also have a strong onion, garlic, or herb scent that arouses the senses. When it comes to preparation, Artisan fries are usually prepared by coating the cut potatoes with four basic ingredients - oil, salt, pepper, and any additional desired herbs, spices, or seasonings.
Many recipes also recommend pre-boiling these seasoned wedges before giving them their final crisp in the oven or deep fryer. Overall, If you are looking for something more flavorful than your standard french fry experience, Artisan fries are an excellent option for a delicious side dish.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Oil (vegatables or sunflower)
  • Herbs
  • Spices
  • salt to taste

Steps:

  1. Preheat the oven to 450°F (230°C).
  2. Wash and peel the potatoes, and cut them into desired shape.
  3. Soak the cut potatoes in cold water for about 30 minutes to remove excess starch.
  4. Drain the potatoes and pat them dry with a paper towel.
  5. Toss the potatoes in a bowl with oil and desired seasonings.
  6. Spread the potatoes out on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  7. Bake for about 20 to 25 minutes, or until the fries are golden brown and crispy, flipping them halfway through.
  8. Remove from the oven and sprinkle with additional salt or seasoning, if desired.
